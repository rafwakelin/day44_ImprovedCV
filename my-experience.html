Volunteering Experience

Growing up, volunteering was always an important part of my life. I spent a lot of time at my church, Perfect Liberty, where I participated in a variety of activities to give back to my community. One of my favorite things to do was to organize "campanhas do agasalho," or clothing drives, during the winter months to collect clothes for those in need. I also organized "campanhas do alimento," or food drives, during times of economic hardship to help alleviate hunger. In addition to these efforts, I also participated in city clean ups, where we would sweep the streets and collect trash to keep our community looking clean and beautiful. I even helped to organize Yakissoba and Pastel nights to raise funds for the maintenance of our church.

In my eighth grade year of elementary school, I had the opportunity to volunteer at my public school, where I was responsible for the computer lab. I taught my peers how to use computers and productivity software like the Microsoft Office suite. While most of the kids only attended the classes to play games, I was happy to be able to contribute something positive to their lives. In college, I continued to volunteer in a variety of projects. I participated in an art project to bring culture to underprivileged people, and I even joined a ballet dance group as the first dancer. I organized events for the citizens of a mental institution, providing entertainment and food for those in need. I also participated in presentations in my city and clean ups in protected forests, working to remove plastic from the environment.

After moving to Dubai, I became particularly concerned about the plight of stray cats in the area. I rescued two stray cats in need, Maya and Pandora, and gave them a home with me. I also contributed financially to vet treatments for other stray cats that I couldn't take in myself, and I frequently take sick or injured cats to the vet for treatment. I also make a point of regularly feeding the stray cats in my community.

When I have the time, I also enjoy teaching English to those who are trying to learn the language. I wish I had more time to contribute to even more causes and make a greater impact, but it can be difficult to balance everything.
